Flávio Bolsonaro Talks More About Lula and the Workers' Party on Instagram Than About Jair

An analysis of Instagram captions from Brazil's two leading presidential contenders shows what each candidate talks about most — and who gets more engagement.

By Lara Castelo | July 12, 2026

Brazil's presidential race is also being fought on Instagram, particularly on the account of Senator Flávio Bolsonaro, the leading right-wing candidate and son of former President Jair Bolsonaro.

The data suggest that Flávio is leaning on the same strategy that helped propel his father's rise: taking aim at the Workers' Party (P.T.) and its leading figure, President Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (Lula).

The senator and the incumbent president of Brazil lead polls simulating the October election.

The Workers' Party (P.T), its supporters or its standard-bearer, Lula, came up more often than any mention of the Bolsonaro family itself — including his father.

Jair Bolsonaro is currently under house arrest after being sentenced in 2025 for attempting a coup following his 2022 election defeat to Lula.

Now, Flávio seeks to assume the role of standard-bearer for the right wing and the Liberal Party (P.L.).

Flavio's references to Lula or the Workers' Party supporters were almost always critical. "Telling the truth is a crime under the Lula government", read one caption from January 14.

Lula's account tells a different story. He rarely mentions his chief rival at all.

His captions dwell instead on social policies such as the public health system (SUS), the Prouni university scholarship program, and the Minha Casa Minha Vida housing initiative.

"A house is a nest. It's where people raise their children, where kids make friends for life. That's why I'm always so happy when we make deliveries like today's, through Minha Casa Minha Vida," Lula wrote in a caption on June 10.

Lula and Flávio Bolsonaro also diverge in volume and engagement of posts.

The starkest gap in volume came in March, when Flávio published posts at a rate of roughly two and a half to one compared with Lula.

That same month, Brazil’s Supreme Court granted Jair Bolsonaro humanitarian house arrest. The decision came only at the end of the month, on March 24, and in the days prior, Flávio Bolsonaro maid several posts to call for the measure.

“They are deliberately putting Bolsonaro’s life at risk. There is no longer any justification for failing to comply with the law and granting Bolsonaro humanitarian detention,” said one post.

Flávio’s posts also received higher engagement on average.

The volume of posts from both camps, and the polarization driving the online debate, is likely to intensify as October’s election approaches — particularly if polling simulations that show a second-round runoff between Lula and Flávio prove accurate.

Methodology

This analysis is based on Instagram feed posts with captions published by Flávio Bolsonaro and Lula between January 1 and July 5, 2026.

The data was collected using Apify and classified into topics — such as Lula & the Left, Bolsonaro Family and Campaign & Policy — using an AI-based classification model built in Python that was validated against a human-coded sample. The accuracy level for both candidates was higher than 70%.

Engagement figures reflect the average number of likes and comments per post over the same period. For a full breakdown of the classification criteria and data sources, see the complete methodology on GitHub.
